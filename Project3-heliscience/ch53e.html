Introduction

The Sikorsky CH-53E Super Stallion is the largest and heavies helicopter in United States military. This three-engined aircraft was designed for carrying up to 33 tones of external loads. The only aircraft that can lift other aircraft as heavy as itself.

Aircraft

Development

The CH-53 was the product of the U.S. Marines' "Heavy Helicopter Experimental" competition begun in 1962. The helicopter was deisignated "CH-53A Sea Stallion" and delivery of production helicopters began in 1966. The first CH-53As were powered by two General Electric T64-GE-6 turboshaft engines with 2,125kW and had a mixmum gross weight of 20,865 kg including 9,072 kg in payload.

Variants of the original CH-53A Sea STallion included the RH-53A, HH-53, CH-53D, CH-53G and MH-53H. The RH-53A were used by the US Navy for mine sweeping. The CH-53G was a version of the CH-53D produced in West Germany for the German Army.

Design

Although similar in dimensions, the three engine CH-53E Super Stallion is a much more powerfull aircraft than the original CH-53A Sea Stallion.
The CH-53E also added a large main rotor system with a seventh blade. Super Stallion has a cruise speed of 278 km/h and a range of 1,000 km. It is also fitted with a forward extendable in-flight refueling probe and it can also hoist hose refuel from a surface ship while in hover mode.

Technical data

Avionics and Flight Controls

Rockweel Collins Avionics Management System
Fly-by-wire flight controls
Pilot/co-pilot/crew chief/4th crew
FLIR with helmet-mounted navigational displays
Aircraft Survivability Equipment (ASE)

Power plant and fuel system

Three T408-GE-400 enginees with less fuel consumption, more power and fewer parts that its predecessor.
Auxiliary Power Unit (APU) with pneumatic start.
Integral EAPPS - Engine Air Particle Protection System
