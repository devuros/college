Safety first

There are thousands of people who travel everyday. Men and woman around the world are always at risk, like cars and planes, helicopters are engineered following top safety standards.

Safety is the most important factor in the lifecycle of an aircraft. From the planning phase to modeling and testing phase, production stage and finally maintanance it is nessecary to implement top standards.

Over the years manufacturers have united to inovate much of the approaches to helicopter safety and have produced marvelous results.
One such result is the Fenestron shrouded tail rotor design implemented by Airbus is almost all of their aircraft. It gives the most protection with least performance losses. This has pushed Airsbus to be one of the leading companies in helicopter design and inovation.

The FAA, federal aviation administration is the highest authory for regulating airtraffic rules and regulations and it's their job to see that every standard has been met. Thus they have the final say in every matter related to air industry. Without them one thing is sure, there would be lot more accidents and fatal mistakes being made.

Jobs

Helicopters are made of thousands of parts, it is impossible to make all of these parts in one place. That's why almost every manufacturer makes parts all around the world, and brings them together to build a fully operational unit. They rely of skilled workers to keep the quiality to the maximum.

Engineers and technicans are sort after all around the world. Because the stakes are high, only handful of selected people get to work for companies such as Boeing. Altough the earning is high, so is the stress from having to keep up with the demands of customers and at the same time ensuring that there are no flaws in the process.

Don't get us wrong, it's rewarding and above all amusing so give it all you got!

Team work

Becoming a pilot is simple enough, but that's when the game really starts, studying theory is one thing. Being in a live environment is another.

Advancing in the service as a pilot is not so fast. First you need to have years and years of flying experience and roughly 8000 flyng hours.
These are the best of the best, testing pilots who risk it day by day. That's when all the experience comes to hand, swift and smart decision can make the difference between saving the prototype aircraft and crash landing. Knowing your co-pilot and having strong, professional ties with him is the most powerful tool anyone can wish for.

Sea rescue

This type of mission is the most challenging, it might seem easy enough, but when you take in acount rought weather, gale force winds, it's the last place on earth were you would want to be.

It tests the capabilities of both men and machines, pushing them to the breaking point. Coast guard is responsible for sea and coastal rescues, when it gets tough they save lives against the odds.

Chinook

This is the work horse of United states of America, because of it's tandem rotor configuration, it is capable of carryng emense loads with ease.

It excels in carrying artilleries, vehicles troops for long distance, also it can "land" on steap terrain becase it can control each of it's rotors allowing for only it's back part of fuselage to have contact with ground.
