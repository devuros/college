Overview

In this lesson we will look in ways how to cancel the torque created by the rotor blade. We will only cover anti torque system in the traditional single main rotor systems.

Theory

There are 3 ways to cancel the torque:
First is to have tail rotor on the tail boom countering the torque.
Next is ducted fan or Fenestron®, introduced by Airbus
The last one NOTAR (NO TAil Rotor), this is the newest of all types incorporated into helicopters. It works by having jet thrusts in the tail boom pushing in the opposite direction of the direction the rotor spins, moving the jet nozzles on the tail boom you can control the direction of heading (yaw). It also relies on the main rotor pushing air downword on the tail boom adding to the control of yaw.

Designs vary in cost, effectivnes and safety.
Tail rotor system is the most effective, it's cost is lowest but it is not safe, landing in populated areas like cities is not a good idea. While Fenestron is safe, it is expensive to make and it is not as effective in cross winds as other systems. As for NOTAR it represents a mix of all 3 parameters, bringing a new approach to tackling this problem.

Conclusion

Understanding what advantages and disadvatages in types of tail boom design and anti torque systems work and influence helicopters.
