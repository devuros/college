Airbus helicopters

Description

Airbus helicopeters (formerly Eurcopter Group) is the helicopter manufacturing division of Airbus Group. It is the largest in the industry in terms of revenues and turbine helicopter deliverioes. Its head office is located in Marseille Provence Airport in Marignane, France, near Marseille.

History

Airbus helicopters, then name Eurocopter Group, was formed in 1992 through the merge of the helicopter divisions of Aerospatiale and Daimler-Benz Aerospace AG. The company's heritage traces back to Bleriot and Liore et Olivier in France and to Messerschmitt and Focke-Wulf in Germany.

Airbus and its predecessor companies have established a wide range of helicopter 'firsts', including the first production truboshaft-powered helicopter, the introduction of the Fenestron® shrouded tail rotor, the first helicopter certified for full flight icing conditions, the first production helicopter with Fly-by-wire control system, the first helicopter to use a Fly-by-light primary control system, and the first ever landing of a helicopter on Mt. Everest (achieved by AS350 B3 in 2005.)

As a consequence of the merger of Airbus helicopter's former parents in 2000, the firm is now a wholly owned susidiary of Airbus Group. The creation of what was then called EADS in 2000 also incoroprated CASA of Spain, which itself had a history of helicopter-realted activities.

As of 2014, more than 12,000 helicopters were in service with over 3,000 cunstomers around 150 countries. Europcopter sold 422 helicopters in 2013 and deliviered 497 that year. In 2014, Airbus build a conrecete cylinder for testing helicopters before first flight.
