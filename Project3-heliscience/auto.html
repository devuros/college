Overview

Autorotation occrus if there is engine or gearbox failure. It is a state of flight in which the main rotor system of a helicopter turns by the action of air moving up through the rotor, rather than engine power driving the rotor.

Theory

In normal powered helicopter flight, air is drawn into the main rotor from above and exhausted downward, but during autoration, air moves up into the rotor from below as the machine descends. Autorotation is permitted mechanically because of both a freewheeling unit, which allows the main rotor to continue turning even if the engine is not running, as well as aerodynamic forces of relative wind maintaining rotor speed. It is the means by which a helicopter can lany safely in the event of complate engine failure.

At the instant of engine failure, the main rotor blades are producing lift and thrust from their angle of attack and velocty. By immediately lowering collective pitch, which must be done in case of an engine faulure, the pilot reducees lift and drag and the helicopter begins and immediate descent, producing an upward flow air throught the rotor. This upward flow of air through the rotor provides suffieicent thrust to maintain rotor rpm trought the descent. Since the tail rotor is driven by the main rotor transmission during autorotation, heading control is maintained as in normal flight.

By carefully analyzing the video, you can see that pilot is positioning normaly during descend, giving enough cyclic input to make the nose pitch down, increasing airspeed and maintaining rotor rmp in the safe zone. Thus having great control of high lose.
Only when the pilots comes close to the ground does he induce flare, giving cyclic input to rise the nose up and pulling the collective up to increase the angle of attack of the rotor blades to produce more light, allowing for a smooth landing.

Conclusion

Although looking quite dangerous, autoration is safe, it must be practiced in order for the pilot to have the feel of the aircraft. Only by trying douzen of times can he be sure and confident of landing the aircraf alone (without the instructors presence).
